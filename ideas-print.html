Data context: The Kanban board feature launched Jun 2, 2018 and tripled average DAU. However, DAU has since plateaued at ~11–13 on weekdays. The top card (Kutch-Mueller) changed status 6 times — showing a core group of engaged users actively managing cards through the full pipeline. The goal of these ideas is to turn that engaged minority into a daily habit for everyone.
1
Card Comments & @Mentions
Hypothesis
If users can leave threaded comments on cards and @mention teammates, the resulting notification loop will pull people back to the board daily to read and respond — turning Shiptivitas from a tool you check occasionally into one you open every morning.
Expected Impact
+40–50% DAU
Social notification loops are the highest-proven driver of daily retention in B2B tools. Each @mention creates an immediate reason for a second user to open the app that same day. With 100 users already in the system, even a 20% @mention rate per card move would generate ~97 extra daily visits per week.
What the feature is
A comment thread on every card. Users type inline comments on the card detail view. Typing @ autocompletes teammate names. Mentioned users receive an in-app notification badge and an email alert. The board view shows an unread comment count on each card so anyone scanning the columns can see where conversations are active.

2
Personalised Daily Digest Email
Hypothesis
If users receive a short morning email showing what changed on the board overnight and which of their cards need attention, they will open the app daily to act on their workload — even on days they had no other reason to log in.
Expected Impact
+30–35% DAU
Our data shows DAU has plateaued around 11–13 users on weekdays after the initial launch spike. Email re-engagement is the most reliable way to lift that floor. B2B digest emails routinely see 45–60% open rates, and a well-timed email converts a passive user into an active one for that day. Over 30 days this compounds into a sustained 30%+ lift in the monthly active baseline.
What the feature is
A 9am daily email (user's local timezone) personalised per team member, showing: (1) cards that moved status since their last login, (2) stale cards they own that haven't moved in 3+ days, and (3) one-click deep links directly into the app for each card. No changes to the card schema needed — reads straight from card_change_history and login_history which already exist.

3
Stuck-Card Nudges & Stale Indicators
Hypothesis
If users are automatically alerted when a card hasn't changed status in N days, they will log in to action it — making Shiptivitas a proactive workflow partner that surfaces problems before they become missed deadlines, rather than a passive board users only check reactively.
Expected Impact
+20–25% DAU
The data shows 486 status changes across 200 cards — an average of only 2.4 moves per card over 8 months. Many cards are clearly sitting idle. Nudges turn that idle state into a daily engagement trigger. A higher card completion rate also improves the core value of the product, driving NPS and word-of-mouth to new freight managers.
What the feature is
Cards that have not moved status in 3 days (configurable) get an amber left border and a clock icon showing how many days it has been stuck (e.g. "⏱ 4d"). Clicking the clock opens a one-action prompt to move the card forward. Team leads also receive a weekly Stuck Cards Report every Monday.
